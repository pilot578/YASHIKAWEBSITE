Medical certificates

Types of Medical Certificates

    1. Class 1 Medical Certificate

  • Required for commercial and airline transport pilots (e.g., airline captains, first officers).
  • Typically valid for 12 months for pilots under 40, and 6 months for pilots over 40.

    • 2. Class 2 Medical Certificate

  • Required for private pilot license (PPL) holders and recreational pilots.
  • Generally valid for 12 months for pilots over 40 and 60 months for those under 40.

    • 3. Class 3 Medical Certificate

  • Needed for air traffic controllers.
  • Similar requirements to Class 1 but tailored for ground-based safety roles.

Dgca exams

1. Student Pilot License (SPL)

  • The SPL is the initial step, primarily an oral examination. It tests basic aviation knowledge and communication skills.
  • The SPL allows candidates to start flight training but does not permit solo flights.

    • 2. Private Pilot License (PPL)

  • For a PPL, candidates must clear written exams on:
  • Air Navigation: Covers basic navigation principles, charts, time calculations, and navigation aids.
  • Aviation Meteorology: Focuses on weather patterns, weather reports, and their impact on flight.
  • Air Regulations: Involves knowledge of aviation rules and regulations applicable to private pilots.
  • Technical General: Basic principles of aircraft operation, engine systems, and other technical aspects.

    • 3. Commercial Pilot License (CPL)

  • To obtain a CPL, candidates must pass a more advanced set of exams. The subjects include:
  • Air Navigation: More in-depth navigation principles, flight planning, and instrument navigation.
  • Aviation Meteorology: Comprehensive knowledge
